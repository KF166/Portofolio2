Phase 1 Course Goals


1. Foster the creative habits of mind

While developing these projects, I aimed to leverage my existing knowledge, as seen in "Under the Sea," and to venture into new subject areas, as seen in "Story of Creation." Additionally, I hoped to align each chosen topic with the website features I needed to utilize weekly.

2. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Throughout this process, I picked up several useful tips and tricks from my peers, and after participating in workshops, I began integrating more images into my website designs. This approach using pictures not only enhances aesthetic appeal but also captures attention more effectively than dense text.

3. Think deeply about craft.

In each project, I delved deeper into understanding specific coding functions. For instance, in "Under the Sea," I discovered how to use the marquee code to create text that scrolls continuously. Beyond the classroom instruction, I explored additional resources online to make website features more innovatively and fun. In "Story of Creation," I learned how to use the container class, which enabled me to embed a button into an image.

4. Engage in sustained research and skills development.

My projects were about a variety of topics, from familiar subjects in "Under the Sea" to more challenging ones in "Story of Creation." This strategy pushed me to explore diverse topics, which will be advantageous when selecting a subject for my final project.

5. Learn to present your work

I always strive to create visually appealing projects, incorporating a variety of images to make the websites vibrant and balanced. Overall, my websites are user-friendly and mostly easy to navigate, though "Story of Creation" requires users to engage with the narrated story in order to go to the next page and continue the story.

Phase 2/3 Course Goals

1. Foster the creative habits of mind

While creating eacgh project, I considered how this could help me complete my final project. I found that Adobe Character Animator would be the most useful because it fits the my theme of creating worlds by allowing me to create alien characters.


2. Engage with others in a workshop environment

Throughout this process, workshop has been an important way for me to gain feedback on my work and find a direction for my work. After looking at the some of my classmates, I was motivated to think more about my final project and used the tools learned in class to create projects that may be implemented in my final project.


3. Think deeply about craft

In each project, I learned how to use the different Adobe tool. However, each project gave me an opportunity to explore what I wanted to concentrate my research on. You will notice that in my first two projects were focused on student life until I decided to shift the focus to space like themes, which is the research topic I ultimately settled on.


4. Engage in sustained research and skills development.

Each project developed different skills. The first two projects allowed me to grow my skills with capturing media by considering angle of teh camera and lighting. Meanwhile, the later projects involved generating digital media or using media found online to complete out projects.


5. Learn to present your work

I consider that each project is presented in the best way media can be presented through a screen. Indeed, like all other forms of media displayed through a computer, the ability to enjoy of this media solely depends on your ability to use a computer or other electronic devices, meaning that people with disabilities or greater age may have difficulty accessing my work.
